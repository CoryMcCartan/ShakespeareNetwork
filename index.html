The graph is made up of nodes and edges. Each node corresponds to a single character, and each edge represents an interaction between two characters. The size of the nodes is proportional to the number of lines a character has. A thiker edge means more interactions. The distance between nodes is also roughly proportional to the relative closeness of the characters. Nodes are colored more green if a character has many interactions with others, and more blue if they have fewer interactions. The color of the edges represents the average sentiment of words spoken between the characters, with green representing a positive sentiment and red a more negative one.
Characters are arranged around the outside of the circle. The width of a character's arc represents the number of connections to other characters that he or she has, while the color of the arc represents the number of lines that he or she speaks. Characters are connected to other characters by bands, whose width at each end represents the number of lines that character speaks to the other. The color of the bands is important, too. A blue band represents a soliloquy. A green band represents a generally positive sentiment in the speech, while a red band represents a generally negative sentiment.
